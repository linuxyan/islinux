Privacy Policy

Last Updated: March 17, 2026

Scope of Policy: This privacy policy applies to the main domain islinux.com and all its specialized subdomains, including but not limited to ai.islinux.com, calc.islinux.com, and tool.islinux.com.

1. Data Collection Principles

isLinux values user privacy and data sovereignty. We adhere to the following strict principles:

  • Minimal Collection: We only collect data that is technically essential for service delivery.
  • Local-First Processing: All web utilities (JSON formatters, encryption tools, and calculators) are executed using client-side JavaScript. Your input data never leaves your browser.
  • No Content Storage: We do not upload, store, or share any content you process through our tools.

2. Information We Collect

2.1 Server Logs

Like most web services, our servers automatically record certain information for security and DDoS prevention:

  • Anonymized IP addresses
  • Browser type and user-agent strings
  • Visit timestamps and referring URLs

2.2 Cookies & Tracking

We use necessary cookies to maintain user preferences (such as dark mode settings). We may use anonymous analytics to understand traffic patterns and improve our technical guides.

3. Third-Party Advertising (Google AdSense)

We use Google AdSense to serve advertisements. Please note the following regarding your privacy:

  • Third-Party Vendors: Google, as a third-party vendor, uses cookies to serve ads on this site.
  • DART Cookie: Google's use of advertising cookies enables it and its partners to serve ads to our users based on their visits to isLinux and other sites on the Internet.
  • Opt-out: You may opt out of personalized advertising by visiting Google Ads Settings.

4. Data Protection & Technical Security

We implement industry-standard security measures to safeguard your interaction with our matrix:

  • End-to-End Encryption: Full-site TLS 1.3 encryption for all traffic.
  • Protocol Security: HSTS (HTTP Strict Transport Security) is enabled to prevent downgrade attacks.
  • Integrity: Regular security patches and restricted server access to protect our infrastructure.

5. Compliance (GDPR/CCPA)

Users in the European Economic Area (EEA) and California have specific rights regarding their data, including the right to access, rectify, or erase any personal information recorded in our server logs (such as IP addresses). For inquiries, please use the contact information below.
